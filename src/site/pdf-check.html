PDF Check

PDFs are one of the most common accessibility gaps in public work — untagged documents are unreadable for many screen reader users. This check looks at the three foundations every accessible PDF needs: a tag structure, a declared language, and a document title.

Check a PDF from your computer

Choose a PDF below. It is checked right here in your browser — it is never uploaded anywhere.

Where PDF problems usually get fixed

Most PDF accessibility problems are fixed in the program that made the PDF, not in the PDF itself. Use real heading styles, alt text fields, and table tools in Word, Google Docs, or InDesign before exporting, and set the document title and language in the document properties. The report's next steps point you to the right place for each finding.

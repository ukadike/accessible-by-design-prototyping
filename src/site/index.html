Accessibility Audit Lab

Check your website, PDF, or creative coding sketch for accessibility problems — and get plain-language advice on how to fix them.

Free. Nothing to install. Your files never leave your computer.

Check your website

Websites can only be checked from inside the page itself, so this works with a special bookmark — you set it up once, and then you can check any page you visit with one click.

  1. Drag this button up into your browser's bookmarks bar:

    Audit this page

    Can't see your bookmarks bar? Press Ctrl+Shift+B (or ⌘+Shift+B on a Mac) to show it.

  2. Go to the website you want to check — for example, your own site.
  3. Click the Audit this page bookmark. Your results appear right on the page.
If nothing happens when you click the bookmark

Some websites have strict security settings that block outside tools, including this one. If your results don't appear, that's usually why. You can still check your PDF documents and sketches below, or ask whoever builds your website to run the developer version of this tool.

Choose a PDF from your computer. It is checked right here in your browser — it is never uploaded anywhere.

Check a p5.js or creative coding sketch

Paste your sketch code below (or the whole HTML page it lives in) and we'll look for common accessibility gaps in canvas-based work.

What this tool can and can't do

Automated checks catch many common problems, but not everything. Each report also includes a short list of things only a person can judge — like whether your alt text actually describes the image.

This tool is an accessibility aid, not a legal certification. For the most complete picture, have people who use screen readers or keyboards test your work directly.

Made for designers, artists, educators, and everyone else who wants their work to reach more people. Developers can find the command-line version, source code, and ways to contribute on GitHub.

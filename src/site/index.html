Accessibility Audit Lab

Check your website, PDF, or creative coding sketch for accessibility problems — and get plain-language advice on how to fix them.

Free. Nothing to install. Your files never leave your computer.

Check your website

There are two ways to check a website, and it's worth knowing the honest difference: the quick check below examines a fetched copy of your page, so it's fast but can miss things — especially on sites built with heavy scripting. The bookmark method runs on the real page in your browser and gives complete results.

Option 1: Quick check (fast, partial)

Heads-up: the address you type is sent through a public relay service to fetch a copy of the page. Don't use this for private or password-protected pages — use the bookmark method for those.

Option 2: The bookmark method (complete results)

  1. Drag this button up into your browser's bookmarks bar:

    Audit this page

    Can't see your bookmarks bar? Press Ctrl+Shift+B (or ⌘+Shift+B on a Mac) to show it.

  2. Go to the website you want to check — for example, your own site.
  3. Click the Audit this page bookmark. Your results appear right on the page.
If nothing happens when you click the bookmark

Some websites have strict security settings that block outside tools, including this one. If your results don't appear, that's usually why. Try the quick check above, check your PDF documents and sketches below, or ask whoever builds your website to run the developer version of this tool.

What this tool can and can't do — being upfront

Automated checks catch many common problems, but not everything. Each report also includes a short list of things only a person can judge — like whether your alt text actually describes the image.

  • The quick website check is partial. It examines a fetched copy of your page, and every report it produces says so. Sites built with heavy scripting may show few or no results — that doesn't mean they're accessible. The bookmark method is the accurate one.
  • The PDF and sketch checks run entirely on your computer. Your files and code are never uploaded anywhere. The quick website check is the one exception: the address you type passes through a public relay to fetch the page copy.
  • A clean result is a good start, not a finish line. This tool is an accessibility aid, not a legal certification. For the most complete picture, have people who use screen readers or keyboards test your work directly.
